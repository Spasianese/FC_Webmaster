THE PATHWAY TO ENGINEERING:

Project Lead The Way (more commonly referred to as PLTW) turns education into a more project-based format, a big divergence from the traditional question-and-answer format, in order for students to have a chance to apply their knowledge to identify a problem then find a unique solution allowing them to effectively lead their own learning and understand how education will continue to be relevant throughout their lives and future careers.

The PLTW Pre-Engineering curriculum offers students a pathway to success in the fascinating field of engineering. Practically every man-made item on the planet was created first in the mind of an engineer. Today’s pre-engineering students are the future engineers who will create things that we can only imagine today. The pathway consists of the following courses during high school

In PLTW Engineering, students engage in open-ended problem solving, learn and apply the engineering design process, and use the same industry-leading technology and software as are used in the world’s top companies. Students investigate topics such as aerodynamics and astronautics, biological engineering and sustainability, and digital electronics and circuit design, which gives them an opportunity to learn about different engineering disciplines before beginning postsecondary education or careers.

PLTW uses the following tenets:

Collaboration

Research/Evidence-Based

Problem-Based

Collaboration: PLTW seeks ongoing input and feedback from students, teachers, administrators, and subject matter experts. The work is also informed by current research and experts in academics and industry.

Research/Evidence-Based: PLTW follows Wiggins and McTighe’s approach, Understanding by Design® to develop a cohesive and coherent instructional path for students.

Problem-Based: PLTW designs activity-, project-, problem-based experiences to prepare students to solve problems. This approach creates scaffolding for student learning and provides the rigor and relevance that engages and empowers our students.

Schools offer a minimum of three courses: Introduction to Engineering Design, Principles Of Engineering, and any specialization course or the capstone course.
